Structure

To help you consolidate the Java topics of the week, assignments are structured to test four areas of understanding:

  • Your comprehension of the concepts taught in class (Written Questions - Required)
  • Your ability to execute your comprehension in a programming environment (Core Assignment - Required)
  • Your mastery of the topics in applying it to a different, complex setting (Assignment Extension - Optional)
  • Your discipline in reviewing and practicing the concepts throughout the week (Additional Practice - Optional)

Although the last two sections are not required, they will help in developing a solid foundation for future assignments and topics. The assignments are not intentionally created in a way such that the previous assignments are required to complete the next; that is the result of the later assignments being more complex systems and needing all these core concepts anyway.

Tips

  • Take breaks between assignment milestones. Each core assignment section is split up into sequential milestones so that you can take a break in between each chunk. While many programmers have gone without sleep or coded for hours on end before, it's neither healthy or productive to do so, and the quality of your code quickly declines. Taking a break before the start of each milestone will lead to coding better and happier.
  • Test as you go. Testing can feel tedious and boring at times, but it is the key to having bug-free code and reducing the amount of work you must do for each assignment. The longer you leave a bug in your code, the longer it will take for you to find it and fix it. Testing at the end of each milestone will allow you to localize where a bug could be, and also have the satisfaction of completing the assignment quickly. Spending hours trying to fix your code and finding out that it was a small bug you missed because you didn't test earlier is the worst feeling.
  • Ask questions. Clarifying parts of the assignment you don't understand or are unsure about will ensure that you are doing the homework correctly. As with exams, you do not want to answer a question that is not being asked. Assignments are designed to test your understanding of particular concepts in well-defined areas. While accidentally solving something different sometimes reaps great rewards for your understanding, it usually does not provide as much benefit as sticking to the what the assignment specifies.
  • Experiment with your code. On the other end of the spectrum, after you have done all the required parts of the assignment, experimenting with the concepts will further develop your understanding of those topics. Experimenting and having a curious mind is crucial in fostering an environment where you learn the most. Think of a twist you could apply to your current code, or create a different program that uses the concepts of the week in a different way. Remember to paste your assignment code into your submission file before experimenting with your current assignment code.

Submission and Grading

For each assignment, all your code will be compiled into one text file ready for submission. To submit an assignment, send me your completed assignment text file via Discord PM by the deadline, which will be listed on the homepage schedule for every assignment. Your graded file will be returned to you through PM with comments attached, scores for each section of the assignment, and an overall score. If you have a question about the grade you received or any of my comments, please respond to the graded assignment PM with your concerns.

Policies

Pair programming partners are not allowed on assignments, unless otherwise specified inside an assignment writeup.

Cheating and plagiarism are strictly prohibited and will result in a 0 on any assignment and possible expulsion from the course, though it makes no sense to have any temptation to cheat because this class is purely for your own edification.

You may refer to online forums such as Stack Overflow to get conceptual understanding or specific library knowledge, but as always, direct copying, paraphrasing, or sharing of code is prohibited and subject to the same consequences mentioned above.

You may ask questions in the #assignment-help channel on Discord or privately PM me; however, posting code in a public channel is not allowed. You may not share or take intellectual property to or from any other student, which non-exhaustively includes code, debugging strategies, or testing strategies. The #assignment-help channel is designated for clarifying questions and questions about concepts related to the assignments, not for questions and answers about the concepts in the assignment and also in the context of the assignment. This applies not only to the #assignment-help channel, but anywhere you may happen to talk about the assignments.
